Victory Garden Chowder

Learn About Recipe

Instructions

  1. Peel and chop onions.

  2. Cut green beans.

  3. Dice green pepper slices.

  4. Scrape and slice carrots thinly.

  5. Peel and grate potatoes.

  6. Grate cheese.

  7. Melt butter in pot.

  8. Add onions and green peppers. Simmer for 4 minutes.

  9. Add beans and boiling water. Cook for 15 minutes.

  10. Add remaining vegetables and cook for 15 minutes further.

  11. Stir in milk slowly and bring to a light boil.

  12. Add salt, pepper and cheese.

  13. Remove from heat imedietly and stir till cheese has melted. Serve imedietly.

Ingredients

  • Butter 1/4 cups

  • Medium Onions 3

  • Green Pepper Slices 2

  • Green Beans 2 1/4 cups

  • Medium Carrots 3

  • Medium Potatoes 5

  • Boiling Water 3 cups

  • Milk 4 cups

  • Salt 2 tsp

  • Cheddar Cheese 6 oz

  • Black pepper to taste.

Learn More

  Covered in the wartime pamphlet titled "How to Eat Well Though Rationed" by Josephene Gibson, this was another dish in the "soup" portion explicity stated to help stretch ingredients further. Notibly, this dish does not use any meat which was a rationed item.
  The name "Victory Garden Chowder" came from the victory gardens or war gardens many of the ingredients would likely come from. During the second world war, Governments on both sides of the conflict including Canada, Germany, Britain, Australia and the United States encouraged citizens to plant vegetables in parks and their own gardens to help suplement rations and raise morale.
