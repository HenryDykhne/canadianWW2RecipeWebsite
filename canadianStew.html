Canadian Stew

Learn About Recipe

Instructions

  1. Wash, soak, and re-wash the beans.

  2. Cook the beans in a pot for 2 hours.

  3. Add the syrup, pepper, and mustard to the beans. Mix well.

  4. Layer the bottom of a large baking dish with bacon. Spread the top with a layer of beans. Cover the beans with a layer of sliced onions. Repeat the pattern till the dish is full.

  5. Cover with stock.

  6. Cook in oven at moderate heat for 1 1/2 hours.

Ingredients

  • Bacon 3 oz

  • Syrup 1/8 oz

  • Beans 1 oz

  • Onions 1/2 oz

  • Mustard 1/75 oz

  • Pepper 1/150 oz

  • Herbs 1/200 oz

  • Stock 8 oz

Learn More

  Sourced from the notebook of W.J. Johnston of the 13th Brigade of the Canadian Field Artillery. What makes this stew quintessentialy Canadian is the ratio of bacon to beans. Both were staple foods for those on the western frontier of Canada. Canadian Stew combines these together in one casserole type dish which is baked in the oven.
  Canadian stew was a common dish in Canadian households during both world wars.
